# Sweden (SM)

The official ham radio world in Sweden could be considered on two
levels, the national level and the local level.

The national level, is the authorities "Post och Telestyrelsen"
([PTS](https://pts.se)) and the national ham radio operators
association "Föreningen Sveriges Sändareamatörer"
([SSA](https://ssa.se)).

The local level is clubs in many towns throughout Sweden, loosely kept
together with a set of district leaders appointed in the national
organisation.  The district are, by tradition, named after the numbers
in the call-sign, SM0, SM1, SM2, ..., SM7.

# Licenses

Since the 1990s there has been only a single class of ham radio
operators corresponding to CEPT2 licenses. In 2025, a new class with
simpler rules and certification was introduced that lead to an upswing
in interest for the hobby. The new class has some limitations as to
what bands are available.

# The callsigns in Sweden

Traditionally, the prefixes used were SM, SK and SL.  SM for
individual ham radio operators, SK for club stations, and SL for
stations connected to the military.

Since 1990s, the authorities has trusted the SSA with performing the
examination, managing the directory of hams and callsigns, and
issueing callsigns.
The callsigns issued by SSA start with the letters SA.
SAnAAA (three letters in the suffix) for individual callsigns and
SAnAA (two letters in the suffix for club stations).

The new class with simpler rules all have callsigns starting with SH,
SHnAAA (three letters in the suffix).

When a callsign is issued, all digits are reserved for that operator
so if an operator is assign SA1AAA, the callsigns SA0AAA, SA1AAA,
SA2AAA, SA3AAA, SA4AAA, SA5AAA, SA6AAA, and SA7AAA are reserved should
he move to another district.

Traditionally the callsigns have been assigned more or less in
alphabetic order so SM0LAA would be issued a couple of years before
SM1MAA.  SA2AAA assigned before SA3BAA with series SAnYLx reserved for
YLs and SAnOMx reserved for OMs.

Since several years this has been abandoned so for new hams, it is
possible to wish for any free suffix, from the SAnAAA-SAnZZZ-series.

The new simpler callsigns, SHnAAA, when assigned must be a free also
in the SAnAAA-series. When upgrading the ham with SHnAAA will get the
new callsign SAnAAA automatically.

SSA also issue vanity call-signs for many different events and contest
operations. Such call-signs can have any of the prefixes SA, SB, SC,
SD, SE, SF, SG, SH, SI, SJ, SK, SL, SM, 7S, or 8S, and then any number
of digits, and any number of letters in the suffix except for
callsigns except for callsigns that would be possible to
confuse. There might be a maximum length but I don't know what it is.

# Local Clubs

The local clubs are organising events and activities for their members
and to reach new potential hams.  Some clubs also organise educational
activities to help studying for the exams.  The national organisation
is encouraging the clubs to do so, developing books and other course
material.

The activity and direction of the clubs differ very much. Some clubs
are oriented towards contesting, some are oriented towards providing
communication activities for car races and similar events that require
communication. Some clubs are oriented towards DX-ing. This is very
much depending on the interest of the members.


# Outdoor activities

Popular activities that are organised online and in the outdoors is
the North hunt (Kommunjakten), SMFF (Swedens Flora and Fauna), and
SMSS (Swedens lakes).  These are organised in a similar fashion as
POTA and SOTA with hams travelling into a certain county (kommun),
into a certain national reserve/park, or close to a certain lake,
putting up a portable antenna and station and then hunters work them.

QSOs are logged using web services and the QSOs are immediately
acknowledge and points are issued.

This stimulates activities in the great swedish outdoors where thanks
to Allemansrätten, the whole of nature is available to everyone
without prior consent from the land-owner.
